Star N Hero

Activewear & apparel retail — built the production, inventory, and demand system from scratch

What it does

A full production-to-inventory loop for a fashion brand selling across Shopee, TikTok, and live events. Every finished-goods SKU is backed by a Bill of Materials; every production run checks real material availability before it's allowed to start.

  • BOM-driven material engine — each SKU resolves its raw-material consumption automatically; running a production plan recalculates stock, reservations, and a live AMAN / STOK RENDAH / KURANG status before anything is approved.
  • Guarded production lifecycle — DRAFT → DALAM PROSES → SELESAI → POSTED, enforced through onEdit() triggers so status can't be hand-edited once material is reserved or stock has been posted.
  • Custom sidebar app — an HTML/JS sidebar (talking to Apps Script via google.script.run) for creating, editing, and copying BOMs without ever touching the raw data sheet.
  • Forecasting & MRP layer — a separate workbook (17 sheets) computes monthly demand rate per SKU, recommends production quantities, and projects inventory health (dead stock, overstock, capital locked) feeding a live Looker Studio dashboard.

Bedsheet manufacturer — order-to-delivery tracking across five production divisions

What it does

Every order moves through Potong → Jahit → Packing → Kirim → (Konversi) — and each division only ever sees its own queue, while the master Orders sheet stays the single source of truth.

  • Auto-generated order IDs with a Roman-numeral month code (e.g. SOULMATE/VI/26/014) issued the moment a customer and product are entered — no manual numbering, no duplicates.
  • Division-scoped views — Potong, Jahit, Packing, Kirim, and Konversi each get a filtered, auto-refreshed sheet; entering a PIC there writes the timestamp back to Orders and advances the step, with stock-availability branching the flow straight to delivery when no production is needed.
  • Cross-sheet reconciliation — a scheduled job (buildDivisionData) merges Orders with an external "Master Data" spreadsheet, deduplicates by Order ID, and feeds a Looker Studio–ready table with per-division value attribution.
  • Self-correcting edits — clearing a PIC cascades forward, wiping every downstream timestamp and PIC so the step always reflects reality, not a stale checkbox.

Reporting System Design

The layer underneath all five dashboards below — deciding what gets measured, how it's modeled, and how it stays current
5Dashboards designed
10+Source workbooks

What it does

None of Vindes' dashboards started as a dashboard — they started as a question ("which programs are actually hitting KPI?", "who hasn't paid us?") that no spreadsheet could answer yet. The recurring job across all five was the same: define the metric, build the data model that can produce it reliably, automate the parts a human shouldn't have to repeat, then wire it into Looker Studio.

  • Consistent KPI framework — every content dashboard (YouTube, Instagram, TikTok) tracks the same shape of metric: a per-post/per-video "KPI Met" boolean on Views/Impressions and Likes, rolled up into a KPI Success Rate per program/category, so leadership can compare a YouTube show against an Instagram pillar on the same terms.
  • Database-first, dashboard-second — each Looker Studio view sits on top of a purpose-built Google Sheet (Program Performance Database, Master Dataset Digital, Sales/Revenue Database, VtmnD Database) rather than a raw export, so filters like Program, Brand, Category, and Date Range all resolve against clean, deduplicated tables.
  • Built for the questions that actually get asked — Revenue dashboards break by Brand, Program, and Quarter with Target vs. Actual built in; content dashboards break by Content Pillar/Category with engagement-rate-by-follower vs. by-impressions side by side, because "did it perform" means something different depending on who's asking.
  • Maintained solo — from the Apps Script automations to the sheet formulas to the Looker Studio charts themselves, the full stack is designed, built, and kept running by one person across all four business units.

What it does

A Google Sheet database that holds every video across all six shows, with Apps Script keeping the YouTube-side numbers current automatically and a Looker Studio dashboard sitting on top for Overview, Time Series, and a KPI-met view per video.

  • Per-video stat sync — pulls title, upload date, view count, like count, and comment count from the YouTube Data API for every video URL logged across all six show sheets, matched by video ID parsed from the URL (handles Shorts and Live links too).
  • Rolling view-count snapshots — walks the VC D+1 → VC D+30 column range and writes each day's view count into the next empty slot, building a day-by-day growth curve per video without manual re-entry, while always keeping D+30 current.
  • Daily subscriber tracker — a separate scheduled job resolves the channel ID from any logged video, queries the channel's current subscriber count, and appends one row per day to a dedicated Subscriber sheet — guarded so it never double-logs the same day.
  • KPI-met dashboard view — every video gets a pass/fail against both a Views and a Likes target; the dashboard rolls that up into Both-KPI-Met counts, a success-rate trend line, and a per-show breakdown (e.g. Tadaaa!! vs. Sepik Bola) so underperforming formats are visible at a glance.

What it does

A master dataset that normalizes posts from multiple Instagram accounts (@vindes.ig, @vixtape, and others) and TikTok into one structure, broken down by content pillar — Daily Content, Promo Program, Client, FAM, Vixtape Konekt — so performance can be compared across accounts and formats, not just within one feed.

  • Dual engagement-rate view — tracks Avg. ER by Followers and Avg. ER by Impressions side by side, since a post can look strong on one and weak on the other; useful for telling brand-deal performance apart from organic reach.
  • Same KPI-met logic as YouTube — Impressions-met and Likes-met per post, rolled into a Both-KPI-Met rate per category, with a trend line over the reporting window — same framework, applied to a completely different platform's data shape.
  • Multi-account aggregation — TikTok alone covers 170+ posts across Daily Content, Promo Program, and a dedicated show pillar (TA'DAA!), each with its own follower and engagement breakdown inside one dashboard.

Why it's structured this way

Five-plus IG accounts and TikTok each export differently and use different naming conventions for the same idea (a "category" in one is a "pillar" in another). The master dataset's job is reconciling that into one schema before it ever reaches Looker Studio — otherwise the cross-account comparisons the brand actually wants ("which pillar performs best across all our accounts?") aren't answerable at all.

VtmnD Finance & Invoicing System

Client intake through approval, invoicing, and payment reconciliation — for two business units with different document rules
6Linked sheets
3Deal structures handled
2Business units (Vitamin, Vakansi)
What it does

A relational system across six linked sheets that takes a deal from client intake through approval, invoicing, and payment reconciliation — for two business units (Vitamin, Vakansi) with different document and folder rules.

  • Client deduplication engine — normalizes company names (strips "PT", punctuation, casing) to detect existing clients and auto-assign a recurring "Anchor" ID instead of creating duplicates.
  • Tax-aware invoice math — handles three deal structures (NON GROSS UP, GROSS UP, ALL IN) and derives DPP, PPN, and Grand Total correctly for each, including official invoice numbering in Indonesian fiscal format (VTMND/VI/2026/003).
  • Approval-gated workflow — a project can't be invoiced until it's Approved; an invoice can't be paid past its outstanding balance without an explicit override prompt — both enforced in the sidebar logic, not just by convention.
  • Payment term rebuilding — cancelling or adding a payment automatically rebuilds the invoice's Term 1–5 amount/date slots from the live Payment Tracker, so the invoice sheet never drifts from the ledger.
  • Drive + Gmail integration — uploaded NPWP/SPK/CL/quotation files are routed to business-unit-specific Drive folders and renamed by convention; approvals, rejections, and invoice releases trigger templated emails automatically.

What it does
